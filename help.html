How CompareMyReturns Works

Transparency matters. Here’s the playbook behind every result you see — from the data sources to the math and the way we summarize outcomes.

Methodology

We compare a lump-sum buy versus a steady daily investment over the same period. Historic price data comes from CoinGecko’s daily close series, and we normalize every result in your chosen currency using live FX rates from our currency service.

  • Daily close prices sourced in USD for consistency.
  • FX rates refreshed on load so conversions reflect the latest market data.
  • Asset list curated locally to guarantee fast loads and predictable coverage.
Calculations

For the selected period, we compare two portfolios. The lump-sum portfolio invests the full amount on the first day and holds to the end. The DCA portfolio splits the same total into equal daily amounts and buys once per day (for stocks, once per trading day). All returns are computed in USD and then shown in your selected currency.

Lump sum

  • Units = amount ÷ start_price
  • Final value = units × end_price

DCA

  • Daily amount = amount ÷ days
  • Daily units = daily_amount ÷ price_on_day_t
  • Total units = sum of daily units
  • Final value = total_units × end_price

The performance difference highlights which strategy finished higher and by how much.

Summary & Interpretation

The results card surfaces the winner, total value, and gain or loss in your currency. We also highlight the performance gap and note when prices or FX data were last refreshed so you can trust what you’re seeing.

Treat the comparison as a historical what-if — it does not predict future performance, but it does give a clear view of how timing and volatility shaped past outcomes.
